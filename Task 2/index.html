Social Engineering Tactics

Attackers manipulate human psychology. Recognize these common approaches.

False Urgency

Demanding immediate action to prevent something bad (e.g., account closure) or to gain something good.

Authority Spoofing

Pretending to be a CEO, IT admin, or government official to command compliance without questions.

Baiting

Offering something enticing, such as a free gift card or exclusive access, in exchange for your credentials.

Spear Phishing

Highly targeted attacks using personal details gathered from social media to make the scam believable.

How to stay Secure

  • Verify the Source

    Contact the person or organization through a known, trusted channel (like a real phone number) if unsure.

  • Hover before you Click

    Always hover over links to see the actual URL destination at the bottom left of your browser.

  • Enable MFA

    Use Multi-Factor Authentication. Even if they get your password, they still need your second factor.

  • Use a Password Manager

    A password manager typically won't auto-fill your credentials on a fake URL, acting as a great defense.
